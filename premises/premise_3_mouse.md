# Premise 3: The Department Mouse

**Setting:** A mouse is loose in the political science department. It is stealing very specific things. It appears to have a plan. Nobody knows what the plan is.

**Tone:** Cozy mystery. Everyone is more curious than alarmed. The mouse is probably smarter than the department.

**Key rule:** Each chapter is one day in the week. Don't try to reveal the mouse's motives — we don't need to know.

---

## Chapter prompts

Find your chapter number in `PAIRS.md` and write ~5 minutes on the matching prompt. Each prompt is a concrete scene — feel free to name professors, invent office layouts, add bystanders. Don't try to match what other pairs are writing.

**Chapter 1.** Monday morning. Professor Lin's stapler is gone. In its place is a small, neatly arranged pile of breadcrumbs. Professor Lin has not told anyone yet — she wants to see what happens next.

**Chapter 2.** Tuesday. The department printer is out of toner. A tiny set of tracks leads from the toner drawer across the carpet to a hole in the baseboard. The department admin has decided this is not her problem.

**Chapter 3.** Wednesday. The grad student lounge is missing exactly one banana. Only one. Not the ripest. Not the greenest. The one in the middle. Three grad students are standing around the fruit bowl debating what this means.

**Chapter 4.** Thursday. Someone has written on the kitchen whiteboard: *"DO NOT TRUST THE HAMSTER."* Nobody knows what this means. Nobody in the department has a hamster. The handwriting is very small.

**Chapter 5.** Friday. Professor Okonkwo's glasses have disappeared from her desk. She was wearing them ten minutes ago. She has retraced her steps twice. Under her keyboard is a single sunflower seed.

**Chapter 6.** Saturday. A grad student has come in to catch up on work. From inside the locked supply closet, she can hear typing. The typing pauses when she approaches. It resumes when she steps back.

**Chapter 7.** Sunday. The department Slack pings. A single message from an account nobody recognizes, with a mouse emoji for a profile picture: *"mouse has demands. will share soon."* The message has 14 reactions.

**Chapter 8.** Monday again. Where Professor Lin's stapler used to be, there is now a typed note. It lists grievances. It cites three papers. It is signed. The signature is a paw print.
