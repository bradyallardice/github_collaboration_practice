# Premise 1: The Conference From Hell

**Setting:** The annual political science conference has begun at a mid-tier hotel in a city nobody chose. Something is slowly going wrong at every session, break, and hallway encounter. Each chapter is one of those moments.

**Tone:** Absurd, deadpan. The characters treat the chaos as mildly inconvenient.

**Key rule:** Each chapter stands alone. Don't try to explain earlier chapters or set up later ones.

---

## Chapter prompts

Find your chapter number in `PAIRS.md` and write ~5 minutes on the matching prompt. Each prompt is a concrete scene — feel free to riff, add detail, invent characters. Don't try to match what other pairs are writing.

**Chapter 1.** The opening plenary speaker arrives 20 minutes late, takes the stage, says *"Before I begin, I need to tell you something important,"* and is immediately cut off by the hotel's fire drill.

**Chapter 2.** A grad student hides under the coffee-break table to avoid their former advisor, who has spotted them from across the room and is walking over with an expression the grad student recognizes.

**Chapter 3.** In the poster session, one poster is slowly but unmistakably moving down the row of its own accord. Nobody wants to be the person who points this out.

**Chapter 4.** Two senior professors are arguing loudly in the hallway about R versus Python. One of them is holding a chair. The conference organizer is pretending to look at their phone.

**Chapter 5.** A panel titled "The Future of the Discipline" has been hijacked by one participant who has started reading their own unpublished manuscript aloud. The moderator has given up. The audience is taking notes.

**Chapter 6.** The conference Wi-Fi password is the same as last year. Someone has noticed. They have already started writing a working paper about it. They are looking for coauthors.

**Chapter 7.** At the closing reception, the open bar has run out of everything except kombucha. An emeritus professor is attempting to negotiate with the bartender. The bartender is not moved.

**Chapter 8.** The conference swag bag contains an unmarked USB stick. Nobody knows what is on it. Everyone has plugged it into their laptop. A hush is starting to spread across the room.
