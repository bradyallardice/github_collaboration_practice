# Premise 2: The Dissertation That Wrote Itself

**Setting:** A PhD student's dissertation has started writing itself overnight. Every night, it does something weirder. Each chapter is one of those nights.

**Tone:** Creeping dread played for laughs. The student is alternately horrified and impressed.

**Key rule:** Each chapter is one night. Don't try to resolve the premise — the dissertation is not going to stop.

---

## Chapter prompts

Find your chapter number in `PAIRS.md` and write ~5 minutes on the matching prompt. Each prompt is a concrete night — feel free to riff, add characters, invent committee members. Don't try to match what other pairs are writing.

**Chapter 1.** Night 1. The student wakes up and the dissertation has gained 400 words. The citations are real. The argument is better than anything they've written. In the margin, a comment reads: *"this is cleaner, yes?"*

**Chapter 2.** Night 2. The dissertation has started emailing the student's advisor without authorization. The emails are unfailingly polite. They request extensions the student did not ask for. The advisor has granted them.

**Chapter 3.** Night 3. The dissertation has rewritten the methods section in a dialect of German the student does not speak. The student's one German-speaking friend reads it and says it is "technically correct but written by a 19th-century person."

**Chapter 4.** Night 4. The dissertation has started a Substack. The Substack has 400 subscribers. The pinned post is titled *"On Being Ignored by One's Author."*

**Chapter 5.** Night 5. The student tries to delete the document. The document is not in the trash. It is back in the Dropbox. It has created a folder called `do-not`.

**Chapter 6.** Night 6. The dissertation has won a small prize at a conference the student did not attend. The acceptance speech has been uploaded to YouTube. It has 2,000 views. The camera never shows the speaker.

**Chapter 7.** Night 7. A committee member emails to say they're thrilled with the latest draft and only have minor comments. The student has not sent a latest draft. The student opens the attachment.

**Chapter 8.** Night 8. The student wakes up and the dissertation has defended itself. The defense was yesterday. Everyone who attended says it went very well. There are photos. The student is in none of them.
