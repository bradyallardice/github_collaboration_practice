# Characters

_Premise: **The Department Mouse.** A mouse is loose in the political science department, stealing very specific things. It appears to have a plan. Feature at least one of these characters in your chapter._

---

### Professor Lin
Senior faculty, comparative politics. The first to notice something was off — her stapler vanished and left a tidy pile of breadcrumbs behind. Rather than report it, she has decided to *watch*. Keeps a private log of the disappearances in a green notebook. Treats the whole affair as the most interesting research question of her career.

### Professor Okonkwo
Political theory, perpetually misplacing things even before the mouse arrived, which makes her an unreliable witness to her own losses. Lost her glasses off her own desk and found a sunflower seed under her keyboard. Skeptical that there's a mouse at all; suspects a prankster grad student. Quotes Hobbes when annoyed.

### Marisol Reyes
Department admin, the only person who actually knows where anything is. Has firmly decided the mouse "is not her problem," a position she restates roughly hourly while quietly leaving out crumbs to see where they go. Controls the supply closet keys. Nothing happens in the building she doesn't eventually find out about.

### Dev Patel
Third-year PhD student, lives in the grad lounge, runs on cold brew and grievance. Convinced the missing banana — the middle one, not the ripest — *means something*. Has started a shared spreadsheet tracking every theft with timestamps. Wants to coauthor a working paper about the mouse. Looking for collaborators.

### Professor Halvorsen
Emeritus, comes in three days a week to "use the good printer." Refuses to believe any of it and blames the department's aging infrastructure. Once cornered the toner tracks with a magnifying glass and declared them "inconclusive." Secretly delighted to have a mystery to be wrong about.

### The Mouse
Never seen directly. Leaves breadcrumbs, tracks, single sunflower seeds, and — increasingly — typed notes. Takes specific things, never random ones. Appears to have a plan, possibly a manifesto. Cites its sources. Signs with a paw print. We do not need to know its motives.
