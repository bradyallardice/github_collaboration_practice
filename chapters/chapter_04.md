# Chapter 4

The mouse had not meant to knock over Gemma's coffee.

In its defense, it had been trying to reach the sticky note she'd left on page 47 of her draft — a note that read *"develop this argument!!"* with two underlines, which suggested she had strong feelings but no follow-through. The mouse had follow-through. The mouse had *footnotes*.

The coffee spread across the desk in a slow, catastrophic arc. Gemma's draft soaked up three pages' worth of her second chapter. The mouse froze.

Then it grabbed the sticky note, tucked it under its arm, and ran.

Behind it, it heard Gemma walk back into the room, pause, and say, very quietly, "Okay. Fair."

The mouse did not look back. It had a nest to finish and an argument to make, and the sticky note — *develop this argument!!* — was exactly the kind of methodological self-awareness it had been looking for. It would go between the red pen and the index cards, right where the gap had been.

The argument was almost complete. It just needed one more thing.

It had its eye on Dr. Wren's leather notebook.
