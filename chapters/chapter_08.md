# Chapter 8

By 4 p.m., Tamsin Quill had stopped updating her clipboard. The walkie-talkie had gone silent—either because all the problems had been solved or because she'd thrown it across the green room. No one dared ask which.

Bethany Crisp had abandoned her poster an hour ago and could now be found at the hotel bar, regaling anyone within earshot with her research. A man in a blazer was nodding politely, slowly backing away.

Meanwhile, in the main auditorium, Dr. Prudence Wexley-Thorne was attempting to deliver an unscheduled impromptu lecture from the podium to three attendees who were too polite to leave. Gerald the AV Guy sat in the back row with a coffee, watching the projector display the word "BUFFERING" for the fourteenth time. He had clocked out emotionally around noon.

Marcus Halberstadt raised his hand. "This is more of an observation than a question," he began, settling in, "but..."

Tamsin's eye twitched. She very deliberately did not reach for the fire alarm.
