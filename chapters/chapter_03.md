# Chapter 3

By hour six, Bethany Crisp had eaten all the snacks.

The granola bars were gone by noon. The trail mix followed around two. The emergency chocolate she kept in her laptop bag for "real emergencies" had not survived the three o'clock panel on post-structural frameworks in contemporary blah blah blah.

Her poster — Marginal Voices at the Margins: A Marginal Analysis — hung perfectly level between the coffee station and the door to the men's restroom. She had used a laser level. She had laminated the abstract. She had printed a QR code that linked to her pre-print, her email, and a Calendly link with 47 open slots.

No one had stopped. Not once.

Forty-three people had refilled their coffee. Nineteen of them had made eye contact with her poster. Fourteen had looked away before she could speak. Four had looked directly at her and still looked away. One man had said "nice work" while clearly reading a different poster entirely.

At 4:47 PM, a conference attendee drifted too close.

Bethany straightened. Her lanyard swayed. Something shifted behind her eyes.

"Hi," she said. "Can I tell you about my methodology?"

The attendee had only wanted more coffee.

They did not get more coffee.
