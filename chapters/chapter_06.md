# Chapter 6

In the high-tech corridors of the Artificial Intelligence Department, Tom was far from a typical rodent. He didn't care for the silicon scraps or the discarded sandwiches in the lab. Tom was a literate mouse with a digital dream, and he was currently executing a heist that would make any hacker jealous.His plan was surgical. On Monday, he bypassed the motion sensors to steal a micro-SD card containing a neural network prototype. On Tuesday, he dragged a high-end GPU cooling fan into the vents. By Wednesday, the head researcher was losing his mind over a missing copper heat pipe and a USB-C adapter."It's almost as if the thief knows exactly which components are compatible," Dr. Turing muttered, staring at a monitor that showed a strange bypass in the server's security.Deep within the ventilation shafts, Tom's nest looked less like a home and more like a server room. Using his knowledge of hardware architecture, he was building his own "Mini-AI." He used the stolen cooling fan to keep his makeshift processor from melting through the floorboards. The micro-SD card held the brain, and the copper pipes acted as a primitive but effective heat sink.His ultimate goal? He wasn't just a mouse; he wanted to be the first Sentient Rodent. He was tired of human experiments and wanted to automate the search for the perfect snack. As the scientists upstairs scrambled to find their missing hardware, Tom sat before his glowing terminal, his tiny paws hovering over a custom-built keyboard. He typed the first command of his new life:sudo run evolution_v1.0.shThe screen flickered, and Tom's eyes reflected the green code. He had the plan, the hardware, and finally, the power.
