# Chapter 5

Gemma Fitch had left forty-three pages of her third chapter outside her office door overnight. By morning, pages eleven through nineteen were gone.

She crouched on the hallway floor and counted the remaining pages twice. Pages eleven through nineteen: the section on methodological ambiguity. She had been uncertain about it herself.

"Peer review," she said aloud, and felt, for the first time in months, a tentative calm.

She brought the surviving pages inside and opened her laptop. In the margin of page ten — the last page before the gap — there was a small mark she hadn't made. A circle, precisely drawn, around a single sentence: *The archive cannot account for what it did not think to keep.*

She stared at it for a long time.

Then she opened a new document, typed *Chapter 4, Revised*, and began to write.

Dr. Tobias Wren passed her open door an hour later, noted the focused silence, and added a line to his leather notebook: *Fitch. Page eleven onward. Something has been resolved.*

He did not disturb her.
